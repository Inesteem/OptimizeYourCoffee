# Coffee Varieties & Cultivars

## Species Overview

| Species | Scientific Name | % of World Production | Characteristics |
|---------|----------------|----------------------|-----------------|
| **Arabica** | Coffea arabica | ~60-70% | Complex flavor, lower caffeine, higher acidity, grows at altitude |
| **Robusta** | Coffea canephora | ~30-40% | Bitter, high caffeine (2x Arabica), disease resistant, used in blends |
| **Liberica** | Coffea liberica | <2% | Large cherries, smoky/woody, jackfruit-like aroma, grown in Philippines/Malaysia |
| **Excelsa** | Coffea liberica var. dewevrei | <1% | Tart, fruity, complex, technically a Liberica variant |

## Arabica Varieties

### Bourbon Lineage
Sweet, balanced — one of the two major branches from original Ethiopian stock.

| Variety | Origin | Flavor Profile | Espresso Notes |
|---------|--------|---------------|----------------|
| **Bourbon** | Reunion Island | Caramel, chocolate, stone fruit | Foundation of specialty; sweet and balanced |
| **Red Bourbon** | Brazil/Cuba | Caramel, chocolate, nuts, red fruits | Excellent for milk drinks; consistent sweetness |
| **Yellow Bourbon** | Brazil | Honey, stone fruit, creamy | Slightly sweeter than Red; yellow cherries |
| **Pink Bourbon** | Colombia | Floral, tropical, juicy | Rare; highly sought after; limited availability |
| **Orange Bourbon** | El Salvador | Sweet, fruity, balanced | Uncommon mutation; similar to Red but brighter |
| **Caturra** | Brazil | Citrus, caramel, mild cocoa | Natural Bourbon mutation; compact plant; easy to dial in |
| **Villa Sarchi** | Costa Rica | Honey, citrus, bright | Bourbon mutation adapted to high altitude |
| **Pacas** | El Salvador | Chocolate, honey, balanced | Bourbon mutation; parent of Pacamara |
| **Tekisic** | El Salvador | Sweet, clean, balanced | Improved Bourbon selection |

### Typica Lineage
Clean, bright — the other major branch. Ancestral to most specialty coffees.

| Variety | Origin | Flavor Profile | Espresso Notes |
|---------|--------|---------------|----------------|
| **Typica** | Yemen/Ethiopia | Citrus, honey, cacao | Original cultivar; prized for clarity and terroir |
| **Blue Mountain** | Jamaica | Sweet, mild, balanced, low acidity | Very expensive; smooth; often imitated |
| **Kona Typica** | Hawaii | Nutty, chocolate, mild | Hawaiian terroir expression of Typica |
| **Maragogype** | Brazil | Chocolate, floral, mild | Very large beans ("elephant beans"); low yields |
| **Kent** | India | Spicy, earthy, balanced | Selected from Typica in India; disease resistant |
| **Pache** | Guatemala | Chocolate, stone fruit | Typica mutation adapted to medium altitude |

### Ethiopian Heirlooms & Landraces
Genetically diverse, often labeled generically. Complex floral and fruity notes.

| Variety | Origin | Flavor Profile | Espresso Notes |
|---------|--------|---------------|----------------|
| **Heirloom** | Ethiopia | Jasmine, bergamot, blueberry, stone fruit | Variable; high acidity; rewards careful dialing |
| **Wush Wush** | Ethiopia | Floral, tea-like, delicate | Named after town; very complex |
| **Dega** | Ethiopia | Fruity, floral | JARC released variety |
| **Kurume** | Ethiopia | Berry, chocolate | JARC released variety |
| **Wolisho** | Ethiopia | Citrus, floral, tall trees | Sidamo heirloom |
| **74110, 74112, 74148, 74158** | Ethiopia | Various | JARC selections; disease resistant |

Regional names (often used as variety on bags):
- **Yirgacheffe** — floral, citrus, tea-like (Gedeo zone)
- **Guji** — fruity, berry, complex (Oromia region)
- **Sidamo/Sidama** — berry, citrus, wine-like
- **Harrar** — fruity, wine-like, blueberry (dry-processed)
- **Limu** — wine-like, spicy, balanced
- **Djimmah** — earthy, wild, less refined

## Processing Methods

### Core Methods

| Method | What Happens | Flavor Impact | Extraction Notes |
|--------|-------------|---------------|-----------------|
| **Washed** | Skin removed, fermented in water, washed clean | Clean, bright, citrus, tea-like | Most predictable; emphasizes origin |
| **Natural** | Whole cherry dried intact 2-4 weeks | Sweet, wine-forward, dark fruit | Heavy body; lower acidity; unpredictable |
| **Honey** | Skin removed, mucilage left during drying | Balanced sweetness, caramel | Milder than natural; forgiving |

### Honey Variants (by mucilage retention)

| Method | Mucilage | Flavor | Compared to |
|--------|----------|--------|------------|
| **White Honey** | <25% | Clean, bright, subtle sweet | Close to washed |
| **Yellow Honey** | 25-50% | Citrus, stone fruit, light caramel | Between washed and honey |
| **Red Honey** | 50-75% | Round sweetness, red fruit | Middle ground |
| **Black Honey** | 75-100% | Intense, blackberry, syrupy | Close to natural |

### Fermentation Methods

| Method | Process | Flavor | Notes |
|--------|---------|--------|-------|
| **Anaerobic** | Sealed oxygen-free tanks | Wine-y, boozy, jammy, funky | Intense; shorter shots recommended |
| **Anaerobic Natural** | Natural in anaerobic tanks | Very fruity, fermented | Experimental; intense |
| **Anaerobic Washed** | Washed with anaerobic step | Clean but complex, floral | More controlled |
| **Carbonic Maceration** | CO2-rich sealed tanks (like wine) | Floral, candy-like, strawberry | Smooth; shorter pulls bring brightness |
| **Lactic Process** | Lactic acid bacteria fermentation | Creamy, yogurt-like, smooth | Distinctive tangy smoothness |

### Regional Methods

| Method | Region | Flavor | Notes |
|--------|--------|--------|-------|
| **Wet Hulled** | Indonesia (Giling Basah) | Earthy, herbal, tobacco, cedar | Muted acidity; heavy body; acquired taste |
| **Semi-Washed** | Various | Mild, balanced, low acidity | Between washed and honey |
| **Double Washed** | Rwanda, Burundi | Very clean, bright, transparent | Maximum clarity |

### Decaf Methods

| Method | Process | Impact on Flavor |
|--------|---------|-----------------|
| **Swiss Water** | Water-based, no chemicals | Best flavor preservation |
| **CO2 Process** | Supercritical CO2 extraction | Good flavor retention; expensive |
| **EA (Ethyl Acetate)** | Sugarcane-derived solvent | Can add slight sweetness |
