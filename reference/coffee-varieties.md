# Coffee Varieties & Cultivars

Sources: World Coffee Research catalog, SCA, specialty coffee industry.

## Major Families

### Typica lineage
Core foundational variety from which many others descend.
- Typica, Blue Mountain, Kona Typica, Kent, S795, Jember, Java, Javanica, Pache, Pache Colis, Pluma Hidalgo, Maragogype (large bean mutation)

### Bourbon lineage
Sweet, complex flavor profile — the other major branch from original Ethiopian stock.
- Bourbon, Red/Yellow/Pink/Orange Bourbon, Caturra, Red/Yellow Caturra, Pacas, Villa Sarchi, Tekisic, Villalobos, Laurina/Pointu (low caffeine)

### Hybrids & Crosses
- Mundo Novo (Typica x Bourbon)
- Catuai / Red Catuai / Yellow Catuai (Mundo Novo x Caturra)
- Pacamara (Pacas x Maragogype) — large beans, complex
- Maracaturra (Maragogype x Caturra)
- Castillo, Colombia, Tabi — Colombian disease-resistant hybrids
- Catimor, Sarchimor — Timor Hybrid crosses (disease resistant, sometimes lower cup quality)
- Obata, Icatu, Catucai — Brazilian hybrids
- H1, Centroamericano, Marsellesa, Starmaya — modern F1 hybrids

### Ethiopian Heirlooms & Landraces
Genetically diverse, often labeled generically as "Heirloom."
- Ethiopian Heirloom, Wush Wush, Dega, Kurume, Wolisho, Badessa, Khudumi
- Regional: Yirgacheffe, Guji, Limu, Djimmah, Harrar, Amaro
- JARC selections: 74110, 74112, 74148, 74158

### Kenyan / East African
- SL28, SL34 — Scott Labs selections, known for bright acidity
- K7, Ruiru 11, Batian
- N39

### Specialty / Rare
- Gesha/Geisha — floral, tea-like, most prized variety
- Sidra — gaining popularity, complex
- Chiroso — Colombian variety, fruity
- Sudan Rume / Rume Sudan — wild variety, complex
- Mokka / Moka Matari — Yemeni, small beans
- Eugenioides — different species, very sweet, low caffeine

### Indonesian
- Sidikalang, Rasuna, Ateng, Toraja, Kintamani

### Other Species
- Robusta (Coffea canephora) — bitter, high caffeine, used in blends
- Liberica — rare, large cherries, distinct flavor
- Excelsa — sometimes classified under Liberica, fruity/tart
- Racemosa — wild species, very low caffeine

## Processing Methods

| Method | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| Washed | Cherry skin removed, fermented in water to remove mucilage, clean bright flavors |
| Natural | Dried whole cherry, fruity/winey flavors, heavier body |
| Honey | Skin removed, some mucilage left during drying (Black/Red/Yellow/White = amount of mucilage) |
| Anaerobic | Fermented in sealed oxygen-free tanks, intense/unusual flavors |
| Carbonic Maceration | CO2-saturated fermentation (borrowed from wine), juicy/complex |
| Wet Hulled | Indonesian method (Giling Basah), earthy/herbal/low acidity |
| Semi-Washed | Partial mucilage removal, between washed and honey |
| Double Washed | Washed twice for extra clean cup |
| Swiss Water | Decaffeination process using water (no chemicals) |
| Lactic Process | Fermented with lactic acid bacteria, creamy/yogurt notes |
